two years after protests began in cairo that would lead to the overthrow of hosni mubarak , protesters are once again gathering in tahrir square , reports the ap . led by liberals and secularists , the protesters are now demonstrating against president mohamed morsi and the muslim brotherhood , which they accuse of threatening egypt 's young democracy . hundreds of thousands are expected to march in protests across egypt today . police and protesters clashed yesterday and early this morning in cairo , with demonstrators throwing molotov cocktails and firecrackers and police responding with tear gas , reports reuters . egyptian officials said 25 people had been hurt since fighting flared yesterday . the muslim brotherhood says it will not stage counterprotests in order to avoid increasing the unrest .
