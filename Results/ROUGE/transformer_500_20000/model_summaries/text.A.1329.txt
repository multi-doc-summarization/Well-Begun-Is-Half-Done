furious french workers at a goodyear tire factory have revived the practice of " boss-napping " and seized two managers amid a dispute over the plant 's closure . the site 's director of production and human resources chief are being held at the factory and union leaders say they wo n't be released until workers get a satisfactory response to their requests , which include more generous severance packages , the wall street journal reports . the bosses were held overnight at the plant and refused offers of mattresses and blankets , the ap reports . " things were sometimes animated , sometimes calm , but without any meanness , " one captive plant manager told reporters , while the other said he would not give any statements under duress . boss-napping was more common during the height of the financial crisis and while it is punishable by up to five years in prison , workers are rarely prosecuted for holding managers captive , the bbc notes .
