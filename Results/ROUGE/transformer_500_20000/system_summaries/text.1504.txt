a terror suspect in the death of two australian counterterrorism police officers from the joint counter terrorism team has been identified as abdul numan haider , the 18-year-old man who was shot dead after he was shot in the head , the ap reports . police chief ken lay says he had his passport cancelled , was seen last week with an islamic state group . " there 's certainly information that he was present at the shopping centre in the last week or so with the flag that appeared to be an isis [ islamic state ] flag , " he says . " it 's not an offence but clearly it drew our attention to this person and we had a conversation with this person . " haider , whose family are from afghanistan , had also been associated with the radical islamic group called al-furqan . al-furqan was the " person of interest " who was expected to attend an interview at the endeavour hills police station in melbourne on tuesday , afp reports . he was under investigation by law enforcement after the shooting , which is understood he had recently moved away from the group .
