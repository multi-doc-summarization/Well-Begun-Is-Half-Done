a virgin islands couple will be trading the caribbean breezes of st. john for the four seasons after winning a " win an inn " essay contest . prince rose adams , both 45 and originally from new york city , entered the contest organized by janice sage . the retiring owner of the center lovell inn had won the restaurant and hostelry in a similar writing contest 22 years ago , the boston globe reports . " in short , janice sage was advertising a contest for ‘ dreamers ’ who would never have the chance to own an inn and restaurant , and then handed the prize to just such a business , " says prass collins , who founded the group . sage says the adamses were notified last week that they had been picked by a local panel of judges assembled by sage . they 're closing their restaurant in st. john and expect to be open for business in center lovell 's home . " we have received some complaints , so we are being responsive to that and the contest she sponsored is that the state law allows them to enter a contest that they never had an actual chance of winning , " she tells the ap .
