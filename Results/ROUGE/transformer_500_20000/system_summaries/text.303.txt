a teenager fell from a gondola ride at an upstate new york amusement park saturday night when he hit the ground . the warren county sheriff 's office said late saturday the unidentified 14-year-old girl from greenwood , delaware , is at albany medical center in stable condition with no serious injuries . the accident happened on the " sky ride " at six flags amusement park , about 55 miles north of albany , new york , the ap reports . the ride was stopped by an operator after word was received that there was a rider in distress . the sheriff said in a statement that the safety of guests is a top priority . " there does not appear to be any malfunction of the ride , but we have closed the attraction until a thorough review can be completed , " a park spokesman said . the park says the girl was riding the attraction with a child relative and fell about 25 feet from a stationary two-person car . the ap notes that the park 's owner , who was treated for a back injury , was released from a hospital . ( a new york city man was arrested after he attempted to catch the falling girl . )
