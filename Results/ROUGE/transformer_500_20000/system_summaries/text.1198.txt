bp has filed a lawsuit against a texas lawyer who claims to represent tens of thousands of clients who turned out to be “ phantoms. ” in a lawsuit filed in federal district court in new orleans , the oil giant is accused of driving up its settlement costs in the 2010 gulf coast oil spill , the new york times reports . mikal watts , one of the most influential attorneys in the country , says it relied on “ brazen fraud ” and “ another of a series of efforts to walk away from the settlement to which it agreed. ” the company says it plans to stop payments and reclaim some of the unspent money . “ the facts of this case shout fraud , ” a lawyer for mr. watts tells the times . “ tens of mikal watts ’ clients have proved to be phantoms ” watts says he never committed identity theft and did not defraud bp or anyone else , the suit claims . bp is seeking a $ 2.3 billion fine , but it 's not clear whether the lawsuit will be filed against the plaintiffs , who are seeking more than $ 75,000 in damages .
