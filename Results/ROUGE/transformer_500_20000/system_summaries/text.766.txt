the 12 boys and their soccer coach trapped in a flooded cave in thailand have been found alive , the ap reports . authorities say they are committed to " 100 % safety " when they consider how to extract a youth soccer team from the partially flooded cave . " we will not rush to take the lads out of the cave , " the governor of chiang rai said tuesday . " more heavy rain could see water levels rise and threaten the air pocket where the group has taken refuge . " the boys were found in the 10th day of a desperate search for the boys , who were found alive in a serious condition . " they have been fed with easy-to-digest , high-energy food with vitamins and minerals , under the supervision of a doctor , " chiang rai governor narongsak <unk> tells reporters . he says other efforts will continue , such as draining water from the cave and exploring the mountainside for the next few days . the group says it is working to swim using special breathing masks . rescuers say they have received their first food and medical treatment in 10 days , but they say the boys are still trapped . rescuers are still searching for survivors and their coach , who is in a coma .
