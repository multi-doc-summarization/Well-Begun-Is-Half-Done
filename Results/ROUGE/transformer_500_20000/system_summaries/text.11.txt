the coast guard says it 's looking for a man who cost the service about $ 500,000 after responding to nearly 30 of his fake distress calls , the ap reports . in a press release , the caller says the 28 calls were made on the night of july 21 and the early morning of july 22 , 2014 . the caller repeats a word used to indicate distress at sea , putting both the public and the responding crews at risk . " a hoax call is a deadly and serious offense , " says lt. cmdr . sara wallace of the maryland coast guard . " calls like these not only put our lives at risk , but they put the lives of the public . " the ap notes that hoax calls is a felony , punishable by six years in prison , $ 250,000 criminal and a reimbursement to a coast guard . the $ 10,000 fine is expected to cost the taxpayers $ 500,000 .
