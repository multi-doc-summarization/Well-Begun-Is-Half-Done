a new study has found that 110 babies who were found to have autism at age 3 are less likely to develop autism , the bbc reports . researchers at the children 's healthcare of atlanta found that signs of autism are measurable and observable within the first months of life . the study , published in nature , found that children with autism have impaired communication and social skills and often exhibit repetitive behaviors . " these early markers are extremely important for us to identify - the earlier we can diagnose a child who has one of these disorders as autism , " study author warren jones tells healthday . " these are the earliest signs of ever observed , " she says . the study found that infants who later developed autism began spending less time looking at people 's eyes beginning at 2 months and continuing until 24 months . the researchers say the difference was not apparent at birth , but the researchers believe it may be associated with symptom . the study also points to a growing number of autism deaths in the us and canada . researchers say that while it 's unclear if a child 's autism can be cured , it 's not clear how long the baby 's eyes are . the researchers hope to raise hope for early interventions .
