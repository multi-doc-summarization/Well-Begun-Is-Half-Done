it 's one of the stranger outcomes of election night : the race for vermont governor is so close that the state legislature might have to decide the winner , reports ap . more precisely , the state constitution stipulates that if no candidate wins more than 50 % of the vote , lawmakers must pick one . that applies here , with democrat incumbent peter shumlin at 46.7 % and republican challenger scott milne at 45 % , a difference of about 2,500 votes . necn points that in in modern political history , the second-place finisher has generally conceded rather than send the vote to the legislature , but milne has shown no sign of doing so . last time this happened ? in 2010 , when lawmakers picked shumlin for his first term .
