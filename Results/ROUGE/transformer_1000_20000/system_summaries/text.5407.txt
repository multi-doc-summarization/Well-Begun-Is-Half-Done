france 's lower house of commons approved a bill today that would extend the right to marry and adopt children , the ap reports . the measure was voted through the national assembly by 329 in favor of 229 countries , which would allow same-sex couples to marry , the new york times reports . the bill would mark the biggest step forward for french gay rights advocates in more than a decade . " the bill is a transformation of the law , " says the archbishop of paris . " it is not the only nation currently wrestling with the polarizing issue of same-sex marriage . " the move is expected to take effect next month , with the country 's socialist party calling it " one of the most important steps in the world , " the bbc reports . the law is planned for march 24 , and the country is planning to pass the bill tuesday , the bbc notes . the move comes as lawmakers in the lower house have voiced support for the bill , the guardian reports . " i think it 's time to say that it 's going to happen , " one activist tells the times .
