president obama has ordered national security and intelligence officials to draw up a list of potential cyber targets , the new york times reports . according to the guardian , the white house released a summary of the directive 20 issued in october 2013 . the directive was spearheaded by president obama to meet with chinese president xi jinping for the first time since the election . the guardian reports that the directive came to light after xi jinping promised to " identify potential targets of national importance where cyber actions are real and that the federal government is committed to severely damaging " cyber actions . the report also notes that the obama administration is planning a series of cyber teams charged with carrying out offensive operations to combat the threat of an electronic assault on the us to be " integrated with the full array of national security tools we have at our disposal . " according to politico , the pentagon is expected to announce a new list of overseas targets , including the rancho mirage memorial museum and the national security agency . it 's not clear how much the directive will be distributed , but it 's unclear whether it will be made public .
