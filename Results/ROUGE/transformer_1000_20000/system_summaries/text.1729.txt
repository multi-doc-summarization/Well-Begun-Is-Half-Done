two weeks after the haiti earthquake , haiti 's twin brother , gilbert bailly , says he 's ready to keep doing it . " so instead of losing the food , we said let 's cook the food and give it away to people that need it . and then two days after we were running out of diesel , it was like , 'ok , it 's not even enough , ' " he tells the port-au-prince sun sentinel . " it 's been a long time since i have eaten . the water , we find it easily . when they are giving out food to the people , they are fighting for it and sometimes it 's all free . " bailly , 40 , has been served a plate of spaghetti and a cup of water . when the earthquake hit , he lost his house and his job . " as long as i have stuff to give , i am going to keep my ingredients busy , " he says , per the washington post . he says his family is living in a public park with thousands of others , including chicken and spaghetti , and that he 's trying to get additional help through the online social network , which has raised more than $ 110,000 , reports npr . " we are doing that since , " bailly says , adding that he has no plans to spoil before anyone would have the wherewithal to buy a pizza . " it keeps myself busy . it gives me hope . "
