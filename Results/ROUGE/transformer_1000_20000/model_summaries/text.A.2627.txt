italian officials have begun operations to salvage some 500,000 gallons of heavy fuel oil from the stricken costa concordia cruise ship , reports the bbc . dutch salvage company smit is performing the operation , which has been delayed by bad weather and the search for survivors ; the salvage is expected to take 28 days . " we expect the next five days to be good weather , and we will work 24 hours a day to pump out the fuel , " says smit 's salvage master . " hopefully by the end of the week we will have the majority out . " some 15 tanks are believed to hold 84 % of the fuel aboard , notes the ap .
