investigators will continue monday combing at the south carolina property of a man accused of a grisly crime spree stretching more than a decade , the ap reports . todd kohlhepp became a suspect in at least seven deaths after a woman was found thursday chained by her neck and ankle in a metal storage container on his 95-acre property near rural woodruff . his arrest in that case led to his confession in a notorious quadruple slaying at a motorcycle shop from 2003 in the small town of chesnee , investigators say . he 's also charged with kidnapping the woman , and more criminal counts are expected . authorities suspect he killed at least three people other than the motorcycle shop victims . the investigation is expanding to other properties kohlhepp , a real estate agent , either currently or used to own , and those properties are not limited to south carolina . the sheriff 's investigative report says kohlhepp was denied bond sunday during a brief court appearance on four murder charges for those slayings . he said they have not yet identified the remains or cause of death , and the search for more remains will continue .
