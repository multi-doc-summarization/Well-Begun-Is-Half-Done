archaeologists have uncovered a stone tool at an ancient rock shelter in the high desert of eastern oregon that could turn out to be older than any known site of human occupation in western north america . the find was announced thursday by the u.s. bureau of land management , which controls the land on which the site was found . university of oregon archaeologist patrick o'grady , who supervises the dig , says the rimrock draw rockshelter outside riley has not been fully excavated . " the discovery of this tool below a layer of undisturbed ash that dates to 15,800 years old means that this tool is likely more than 15,800 years ago . but what has the archaeological community most excited is a small stone tool found below to scrutiny , it would be the oldest human occupation west of the rockies , and another predating the so-called clovis culture , once generally believed to be the first people to migrate from asia into north america , " the ap notes . the earliest clovis artifacts , known for distinctive and elegant stone points , are dated to about 13,000 years ago , the ap reports .
