german police recovered around 100 items that belonged to late beatles star john lennon that were stolen from his widow in new york , including three diaries of his signature metal-rimmed glasses , a cigarette case and a handwritten music score . " this was a spectacular , unusual criminal case , " police spokesman winfrid wenzel told reporters . german authorities first became aware of the items , stolen from yoko ono at her new york home in 2006 , when a bankruptcy administrator for the berlin auction house auctionata contacted them in july . the administrator had found the memorabilia in the company 's storage . police confiscated the items from the auctioneers two weeks later , and on monday arrested . detectives said much of the haul was confiscated from an auction house in berlin in july , sparking an investigation to find the rest of the stolen items . ono identified the second suspect used to work as a chauffeur for ono . the items were put on display at berlin police headquarters with other items including a tape recording of a beatles concert , two pairs of glasses , sheet music and a bankruptcy case .
