an off-duty california highway patrol officer is being praised for her integrity after she returned $ 120,000 in cash in the middle of a concord roadway and turned the money over to authorities , the ap reports . the sergeant , who asked not to be identified in the media , was driving in concord , calif. , in september when she had to swerve to avoid two large bank deposit bags in the road . she took a u-turn to pick them up and found a large amount of cash equalling more than $ 120,000 . she reported the money and the police were able to track down the owner . her friend and children could not believe she returned the money but the officer , who was raised by a single mother in los angeles , said it was an opportunity to be an example for her children and teach them a lesson . " on too many occasions our personnel do not pause to be recognized as they feel they were simply doing their job , " the sergeant said . " but that 's what determines a person 's integrity , it 's what you do when nobody is looking . "
