while his mother was preparing food in the kitchen , a 5-year-old florida boy called 911 to invite law enforcement officers over for thanksgiving dinner , the ap reports . monica webster of the walton county sheriff 's office tells the news herald that with all the bad calls they receive every day , this was a happy call . but young billy nolin 's family had no idea he 'd invited guests to dinner . mom landi mccormick says she was cooking when billy 's grandfather noticed him talking to someone on an old cellphone . mccormick reprimanded billy when he admitted calling 911 . he was crying when deputy dannon byrd drove up . she says the deputy thanked billy for his kind invitation , then reminded him he should only use 911 for emergencies . the deputies gave billy a sheriff 's badge .
