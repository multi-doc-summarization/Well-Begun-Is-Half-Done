the bipartisan commission on wartime contracting decried on monday a federal system that has allowed contractors in iraq and afghanistan to commit fraud -- then get hired again and again , the ap reports . sen. james webb , d-virginia , who helped establish the commission three years ago , said friday that its latest proposals deserve attention from congress and the department of defense and called for a hearing on the matter . " the government has abdicated its contracting responsibilities -- too often using contractors as the default mechanism , driven by considerations other than whether they provide the best solution , and without consideration for the resources needed to manage them , " the report says . " these contractors ' deaths and injuries should not be ignored , but should be a part of the public debate on the cost of war . " " for the 200,000 people employed by contractors to provide support and afghanistan , accountability is too often absent , diluted , or avoided , " republican co-chair chris shays said in a statement released with the report .
