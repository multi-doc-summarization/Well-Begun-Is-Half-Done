unicef says girls spend 40 % more time performing unpaid household chores than boys , reports the un children 's agency . unicef said the difference in time spent working amounted to 160 million extra hours a day worldwide . two out of three girls cook and clean in the home , and almost half collect water or firewood . they also perform more " less visible " domestic work like childcare or looking after the elderly , the report says . " the overburden of unpaid household work begins in early childhood and intensifies as girls reach adolescence , " unicef 's principal gender advisor anju malhotra tells a new report . " this unequal distribution of labour among children also perpetuates gender stereotypes and the double burden on women and girls across generations . " ( the report notes that girls between 10 and 14 years old spending 30 per cent more time , or 120 million more hours each day . ) girls sacrifice important opportunities to learn , grow , and just enjoy their childhood , " the report , which also includes data on violence , child marriage , female genital mutilation and education , is being released to coincide with the un 's international day of the girl child on 11 october . harnessing the power of data for girls : taking stock and looking ahead to 2030 includes the first global estimates on the time girls spend doing household chores such as cooking , cleaning , caring for family members and collecting water and firewood . 10 to 14 year-old girls in somalia spend the most amount of time on household chores compared to boys .
