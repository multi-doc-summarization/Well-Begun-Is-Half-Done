two daycare workers were charged in connection to an incident at a palmer square day care in which five toddlers were injured with hot glue.lizandra cosme , 32 , of chicago , was arrested sunday and charged with five counts of aggravated battery of a child under 13 years old causing great bodily harm , the ap reports . a cook county judge ordered her held without bail on monday . she was watching 16 children at the time of the hot glue gun incident , where they were working , on dec. 1 . video footage allegedly showed cosme putting hot glue on the hands and arms of the five 2-year-olds , who appear to apply neosporin to one of them after she saw circular burn marks starting to appear.later in the day , in what appears to have been an effort to cover up what had happened , prosecutors say . " the well-being of our students is paramount , " a prosecutor tells the chicago tribune . " upon learning of the alleged incident , we notified dcfs immediately and terminated two of our employees . we are currently working with investigators . " cosme was arrested with five misdemeanor counts of causing the circumstances of child endangerment , police said . her next court appearance is dec. 26 .
