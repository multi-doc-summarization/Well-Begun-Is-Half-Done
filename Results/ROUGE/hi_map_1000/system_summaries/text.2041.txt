a missouri appeals court on tuesday tossed out a $ 417 million jury award to a woman who claimed she developed ovarian cancer by using johnson & johnson talc-based baby powder for feminine hygiene . los angeles county superior court judge maren nelson granted the company 's request for a new trial , saying there were errors and jury misconduct in the previous trial that ended with the award two months ago . the missouri eastern district court 's ruled that missouri was not the proper jurisdiction to hear a lawsuit filed by jacqueline fox , 62 , of birmingham , alabama , who claimed the baby powder on a daily basis beginning in the 1950s until 2016 and was diagnosed with ovarian cancer in 2007 , about four months before her case went to trial in st. louis circuit court . the appeals court cited a supreme court ruling in june that placed limits on where injury lawsuits could be filed , saying state courts can not hear claims against companies not based in the state where alleged injuries occurred .
