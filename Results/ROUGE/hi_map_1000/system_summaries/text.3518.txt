the us shed 33,000 jobs in september because of hurricanes harvey and irma , which closed thousands of businesses in texas and florida and forced widespread evacuations . it was the first decline in nearly seven years . the unemployment rate fell to 4.2 percent from 4.4 percent , the labor department said friday , ending the longest stretch of job growth on record . but the picture was distorted by hurricane harvey , which rocketed higher in september . traders have long looked particularly carefully at that measure of wages because so many economists have been confounded by the lack of wage growth in the area reopen and construction companies ramp up repair and renovation work . last month 's drop was driven by huge losses in restaurants and bars , which shed 105,000 jobs , a sign of the damage to florida 's tourism industry . roughly 1.5 million people were unable to work last month because of the storms . as a result , the drop in the jobless rate is a sign the job market improved outside hurricane-hit areas . the 0.5 % gain in average hourly earnings versus a month ago indeed shows an accelerated pace , but that 's likely due in part to the effects of hurricanes .
