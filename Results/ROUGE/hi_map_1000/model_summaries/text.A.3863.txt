farmers in iowa who planted corn seeds genetically modified to fend off the dreaded corn rootworm are seeing a troubling sign : the rootworm is apparently developing a resistance to the monsanto seeds and gobbling up cornfields again , say iowa university researchers . it 's still just a small percentage of rootworms that have adapted , reports afp , but the development is renewing fears that biotech crops will create superbugs . " these are isolated cases , and it is n't clear how widespread the problem will become , " an iowa state entomologist tells the wall street journal . " but it is an early warning that management practices need to change . " at mother jones , blogger tom philpott thinks monsanto has put farmers on a " treadmill " of sorts : " the need to apply ever more , and ever more novel , high-tech responses to keep up with ever-evolving pests . "
